Creating a Live Stream Video Portal

The COVID-19 virus is shutting down entire countries, placing millions of people in temporary self isolation to reduce transmission rates.

With thousands of schools, universities and businesses canceling classes and face to face meetings, there has been a huge uptake in virtual meetings. Tools like Zoom, Skype and Google Hangouts are seeing huge upsurges in traffic as more and more daily activities move online and remote.

But, what about students or colleagues who do not have fast internet at home? Zoom recommends 1 MBPS (MegaBit per second) downlink speed for video stream calls. For our collaborators with poor internet, we can’t fall back on “use the internet at the library” when we’re supposed to be staying at home.

Further, as more and more users come onto Zoom, we may reach a breaking point where no new connections can be made. How can we both stream to everyone, but alleviate the strain on the tools that are going to be used the most.

Sharing a Live Stream to Slow Connections

To keep things going, we need a way to live stream ourselves to folks with a slower connections. As a bonus, it also alleviates the number of connections to Zoom.

We can still use Zoom to host the meeting, but we’ll stream the video to api.video, and use Glitch as our webhost to show the live video to our audience.


What are the advantages of this transmission? api.video creates streams at many different qualities, all the way down to 250 KBPS (KB per second). This will allow your viewers to watch your stream on even the slowest network connections.
